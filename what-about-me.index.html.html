The question at the center

Where do I exist
in this relationship?

This platform exists for the people who have been quietly learning to want less. Who have watched their needs get rerouted, postponed, explained away. Who have been told — or come to believe — that their pain is jealousy, their grief is immaturity, their limits are a problem to be solved.

This is not about anyone else. It is about you. What you're carrying. What it's cost you. And what it might mean to return to yourself.

"You're not crazy." "This isn't just jealousy." "This is grief." "This is attachment." "This is about you too."
The Campaign · Archetypes

The ways we learn
to make ourselves smaller.

These are not personality types. They are survival strategies — the shapes we take when love keeps asking us to adjust. Recognising yours is not a diagnosis. It is a beginning.

I

The Patient One

You've learned that love is something to wait for. That your turn will come if you're just steady enough, quiet enough, understanding enough. Patience became a permanent posture — not a choice.

Waiting · Deferred hope
II

The Quiet One

You stopped saying certain things. Not because they weren't true — but because saying them created problems. Silence felt safer than the response. Over time, you stopped knowing what you needed at all.

Silence · Self-erasure
III

The Flexible One

You became someone who could adapt. Who didn't make demands. Your limits softened and moved until you weren't sure where they'd been to begin with. Flexibility as survival.

Boundaries · Adaptation
IV

The Fixer

If you could just solve the right problem, understand the right dynamic, have the right conversation — things would settle. You've been working hard on a relationship that isn't working on you.

Control · Anxious repair
The Course

A structured
path back to yourself.

This is an anonymous, asynchronous online course — therapeutic in tone, educational in approach. It is not crisis care. It is the kind of slow, structured reckoning that most conversations never make space for.

You can move through it at your own pace, in your own privacy, without naming anyone else.

Hosted on Thinkific. No prior experience required. Take it alone. Take it quietly.

Module 01

Naming What Happened

Betrayal trauma, relational displacement, and why your body knows before your mind does. Building language for experiences that don't have easy names.

Module 02

Attachment & Why It Hurts Like This

Understanding your attachment style in the context of relational insecurity. Why the pain is not irrational, and what it's actually signaling.

Module 03

Grief That Has No Name

Complicated grief. Anticipatory loss. The mourning of what you hoped for. How to hold grief that the people around you may not validate.

Module 04

Where Your Limits Actually Are

Boundary confusion, self-abandonment, and the difference between compromise and erosion. Rebuilding your sense of what is and isn't okay.

Module 05

Chronic Comparison & Self-Worth

The particular devastation of being compared — or comparing yourself. What it does to identity and how to separate your worth from the dynamic.

Module 06

The Return

Gentle accountability. What now looks like. Not a resolution, but an orientation — toward yourself, your needs, your future.

Who this is for

If you've ever asked
"Am I too much?"
this is for you.

This platform is for anyone who has found themselves emotionally sidelined in a relationship — regardless of gender, orientation, or the structure of that relationship.

"This is not a platform about what other people are doing. It is entirely about what is happening to you."

You are in or have been in a relationship where your partner has other partners — formally or informally.

You have felt invisible, deprioritised, or like your feelings were always the problem to be managed.

You've been told your pain is jealousy, insecurity, or something you need to work on — while your actual needs went unacknowledged.

You are trying to understand what happened to your sense of self — and where to go from here.

You want to process this quietly, anonymously, without having to explain yourself to anyone.
